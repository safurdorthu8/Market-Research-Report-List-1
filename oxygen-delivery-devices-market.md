<p><p>Oxygen delivery devices are categorized into low flow and high flow delivery systems. Low flow devices, such as nasal cannulas and simple face masks, provide variable oxygen concentrations, allowing for patient comfort and mobility while delivering lower oxygen levels. High flow devices, including non-rebreather masks and high-flow nasal cannula systems, deliver a fixed concentration of oxygen at higher flow rates, ensuring precise oxygen delivery for patients with more severe respiratory conditions. Both types play crucial roles in respiratory care.</p></p>
