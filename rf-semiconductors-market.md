<p><p>The RF semiconductor market encompasses various materials, primarily Gallium Arsenide (GaAs), Gallium Nitride (GaN), and Silicon (Si). GaAs is favored for high-frequency applications due to its efficient electron mobility and low noise characteristics, making it ideal for aerospace and telecommunications. GaN offers high power density and efficiency, suitable for high-frequency, high-power applications like radar and 5G. Silicon, while less efficient in these areas, is cost-effective and widely used for low-frequency applications and consumer electronics, balancing performance and affordability.</p></p>
