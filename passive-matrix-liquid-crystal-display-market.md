<p><p>A Passive Matrix Liquid Crystal Display (PMLCD) is a type of display technology that uses a grid of horizontal and vertical conductors to control individual pixels. Unlike Active Matrix displays, which utilize a transistor for each pixel, PMLCDs rely on a simpler design, making them less expensive and easier to manufacture. They are commonly used in devices where cost-efficiency and low power consumption are prioritized, such as calculators, simple handheld devices, and some small screen applications.</p><p>The market for Passive Matrix Liquid Crystal Displays is witnessing notable growth, driven by increasing demand for low-cost display solutions in various electronic devices. The growing adoption of PMLCDs in wearables, automotive, and consumer electronics further bolsters market expansion. Moreover, emerging trends include the integration of PMLCDs in IoT (Internet of Things) devices, where cost and simplicity are critical. </p><p>As manufacturers focus on enhancing the performance and efficiency of PMLCD technology, the market is projected to grow at a CAGR of 9.4% during the forecast period.
