In contrast, hydraulic UTMs employ fluid pressure to exert force, making them ideal for testing larger and more robust specimens. Both types cater to diverse industries, including materials science, quality control, and research, ensuring accurate measurements of tensile strength, compression, and other mechanical properties.</p></p>
