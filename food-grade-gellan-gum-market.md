<p><p>The food grade gellan gum market is categorized into two main types: high acyl and low acyl gellan gum. High acyl gellan gum is known for its elastic gel properties, making it ideal for applications in desserts, dairy products, and toppings. In contrast, low acyl gellan gum forms a firm gel and is often used in confectioneries, sauces, and dairy alternatives. Both types serve diverse culinary needs, enhancing texture and stability in various food products.</p></p>
